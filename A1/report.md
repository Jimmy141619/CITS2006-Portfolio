# A1: Security Concepts on Campus (Reid Library)

## **Overview**

This analysis focuses on security mechanisms observed at Reid Library (UWA). As a high-traffic academic space, the library implements multiple layers of physical and digital security to protect assets, users, and infrastructure.

---

## Security Measures Identified

###  Device Restrictions (Asset Control / Risk Reduction)

* The library no longer provides public desktop computers; instead, users must connect their own laptops to external monitors.

**Purpose:**

* Reduces the number of shared devices vulnerable to malware or misuse
* Limits institutional responsibility for endpoint security

**Weaknesses:**

* Users may connect compromised personal devices to the network
* Lack of managed devices reduces control over user activity

---

### Access Control (Restricted Areas)

* Certain areas (e.g., staff rooms, archives) are restricted and not accessible to the public.

**Purpose:**

* Prevents unauthorized access to sensitive materials and systems
* Enforces authorization boundaries

**Weaknesses:**

* Tailgating (unauthorized individuals following authorized users)
* Reliance on users complying with access rules

---

### CCTV Surveillance

* Visible cameras are installed throughout the library.

**Purpose:**

* Acts as both a deterrent and monitoring system
* Enables incident investigation

**Weaknesses:**

* May not be actively monitored at all times
* Potential blind spots in coverage

---

### Exit Alarm System

* Security gates at exits trigger alarms if items are removed without proper checkout.

**Purpose:**

* Prevents theft of books and materials
* Automates enforcement without manual checks

**Weaknesses:**

* Can be bypassed if tags are removed or damaged
* False alarms may reduce effectiveness over time

---

### Environmental Design (CPTED)

* The library has an open layout with clear lines of sight.

**Purpose:**

* Encourages natural surveillance
* Reduces opportunities for hidden or suspicious activity

**Weaknesses:**

* Quiet or isolated areas may still exist
* Relies on passive observation rather than active enforcement

---

### Network Security (Wi-Fi Authentication)

* Access to the university Wi-Fi requires login credentials.

**Purpose:**

* Provides user authentication and accountability
* Enables monitoring of network activity

**Weaknesses:**

* Public network environment increases exposure to attacks (e.g., sniffing, rogue access points)
* Users may still engage in insecure behaviour

---

### Emergency Systems

* Fire alarms and emergency exits are clearly installed and accessible.

**Purpose:**

* Ensures rapid response during emergencies
* Protects occupants from physical harm

**Weaknesses:**

* Emergency exits could potentially be misused for unauthorized access or exit
* Systems depend on proper maintenance and user awareness

---

### Human Monitoring (Staff Presence)

* Library staff are present throughout the building.

**Purpose:**

* Provides a human layer of security
* Enables immediate response to incidents

**Weaknesses:**

* Limited coverage across large areas
* Relies on human attention and judgement

---

## Risk Analysis

While Reid Library implements layered security, several risks remain:

* Over-reliance on passive monitoring (CCTV, staff presence)
* Exposure to network-based attacks via public Wi-Fi
* Vulnerability to theft of unattended personal devices

---

## Recommendations

* Improve CCTV coverage to reduce blind spots
* Implement awareness campaigns about device security
* Strengthen access control enforcement in restricted areas
* Enhance monitoring of network activity for suspicious behaviour

---

## Conclusion

Reid Library demonstrates a layered security approach combining physical controls, digital systems, and human oversight. However, like most public environments, its effectiveness depends on user behaviour and active enforcement.
